Method

How records are classified

Events enter the public file only with a named source and an evidence status. Stronger sources override weaker summaries. Wikipedia pages are leads, not primary proof.

Official

Government, court, regulator, or statutory body record.

Court-tested / Convicted / Charged

Litigated outcomes, convictions, charges, dismissals, or other court-tested states.

Investigated

Named probe by police, CBI, commissions, or other investigative bodies without a settled court finding.

Reported

Credible newswire, rights group, or established publication reporting.

Alleged

Serious public allegation that has not reached a settled legal finding.

Index / Affidavit analysis

Rankings, indexes, or affidavit-derived tallies treated as measurements, not courtroom verdicts.

Opposition claim

Political claim preserved with attribution, not treated as primary proof.

Official proposal / reversed

Government proposals, ordinances, or later reversals kept distinct from enacted outcomes.

Dynamic lead

Wikipedia or live pages used only as leads until stronger references are attached.

FAQ

Common questions

How this record is built, what it does and does not claim, and how to challenge an entry.

What is LetsFixIndia?

A sourced public-record timeline of major governance, rights, press-freedom, and institutional events in India from 2014 to 2026, during the BJP/Modi administrations. Every entry carries a named source and a visible evidence status. The archive records improvements too; a criticism that cannot survive a fair comparison is not useful criticism.

Is this an anti-BJP site?

It is a critical record, and it says so openly (or as a popular online comment goes, "the human body is not meant to endure three BJP terms"). But criticism is bound by evidence rules: opposition claims are labelled as claims, allegations are not written as convictions, and indicators that improved under this government — GDP per capita, inflation, internet access, PLFS unemployment — are shown on the Stats page alongside the ones that worsened.

Why does the timeline start at 2014?

Because 26 May 2014 is when the current management took over. The project focuses strictly on this 2014-2026 timeline scope.

Why are some entries sourced to Wikipedia?

Wikipedia is used only as a lead list, never as final proof. Entries that still rest on a Wikipedia citation are being upgraded to primary or newswire sources, and weak leads are held in a research backlog rather than promoted to the timeline.

An entry about me or my organisation is wrong. How do I get it corrected?

Use the Submit page and mark the record as a correction, with source links. Corrections backed by stronger evidence than the current entry override it — that is a standing rule of the project, not a favour.

How is the "BJP at centre" number calculated?

It sums three periods of BJP-led central government: Vajpayee's 16-day term in 1996, the Vajpayee governments from 19 March 1998 to 22 May 2004, and the Modi period from 26 May 2014 to today. It recalculates live on every visit.

Who is behind this, and can I contribute?

The project is maintained independently — see the About page. The most useful contribution is specific: a missing event, a stronger source, a court-status update, a translation, or a correction to wording that claims more than the evidence supports. "Everyone knows" is not a citation.

Does the site track me?

No cookies, no accounts, no ad trackers. We use cookieless, privacy-friendly page-view counting (so we know if anyone is actually reading this) and nothing else. Submission drafts and your timeline reading position live only in your own browser and never leave your device.

Who funds this?

Nobody, which is both the good news and the problem. There is no party, NGO, foreign hand, or mysterious uncle behind this — just one maintainer, Basith (@basithladdu), and a support page that helps cover hosting, media storage, and maintenance time. Money pays infrastructure and upkeep; it has never bought a single word of an entry, and it never will. The evidence rules on the Method page are the editor, not any donor.

How can I help without money?

Share it — that is genuinely worth more than a donation. Beyond that: submit missing events with sources, flag wording that claims more than the evidence shows, check court-status updates, or contribute on GitHub. A record like this stays alive by being read, checked, and corrected.

Can I inspect the raw data or host a mirror?

Yes. The dataset and codebase are open source in the public repository, so contributors can inspect the records, verify the code, run it locally, or host a mirror. The website itself does not show raw-data download buttons.
