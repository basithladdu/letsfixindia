Method

How records are classified

Events are included when they are tied to a named source and a visible evidence status. Stronger sources override weaker summaries.

Official

Government, court, regulator, or statutory body record.

Court-tested

Litigated, convicted, dismissed, charged, or otherwise tested in court.

Reported

Credible newswire, rights group, or established publication reporting.

Alleged

Serious public allegation that has not reached a settled legal finding.

Opposition claim

Political claim preserved with attribution, not treated as primary proof.

Dynamic lead

Wikipedia or live pages used only as leads until stronger references are attached.

FAQ

Common questions

How this record is built, what it does and does not claim, and how to challenge an entry.

What is LetsFixIndia?

A sourced public-record timeline of major governance, rights, press-freedom, and institutional events in India from 2014 to 2026, during the BJP/Modi administrations. Every entry carries a named source and a visible evidence status.

Is this an anti-BJP site?

It is a critical record, and it says so openly (or as a popular online comment goes, "the human body is not meant to endure three BJP terms"). But criticism is bound by evidence rules: opposition claims are labelled as claims, allegations are not written as convictions, and indicators that improved under this government — GDP per capita, inflation, internet access, PLFS unemployment — are shown on the Stats page alongside the ones that worsened.

What do the evidence labels mean?

Official — government, court, or regulator record. Court-tested — litigated, charged, convicted, or dismissed in court. Reported — credible newswire, rights group, or established publication. Alleged — serious public allegation without a settled legal finding. Opposition claim — political claim kept with attribution, not treated as proof. The full rules are on the Method page.

Why does the timeline start at 2014?

Because 26 May 2014 is when the current management took over. Earlier context, including 2002, is kept separately in the pre-2014 archive, outside the 2014-2026 timeline scope.

Why are some entries sourced to Wikipedia?

Wikipedia is used only as a lead list, never as final proof. Entries that still rest on a Wikipedia citation are being upgraded to primary or newswire sources, and weak leads are held in a research backlog rather than promoted to the timeline.

An entry about me or my organisation is wrong. How do I get it corrected?

Use the Submit page and mark the record as a correction, with source links. Corrections backed by stronger evidence than the current entry override it — that is a standing rule of the project, not a favour.

How is the "BJP at centre" number calculated?

It sums three periods of BJP-led central government: Vajpayee's 16-day term in 1996, the Vajpayee governments from 19 March 1998 to 22 May 2004, and the Modi period from 26 May 2014 to today. It recalculates live on every visit.

Who is behind this, and can I contribute?

The project is maintained independently — see the About page. Contributions are welcome through the Submit page: missing events, court-status updates, stronger sources for weak entries, and flags on wording that claims more than the evidence supports.

Does the site track me?

No analytics, no cookies, no accounts. Submission drafts and your timeline reading position are stored only in your own browser's local storage and never leave your device.
