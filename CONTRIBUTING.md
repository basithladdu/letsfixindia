# Contributing to LetsFixIndia

LetsFixIndia is a source-led public record. Contributions are welcome when
they make the record more accurate, more complete, or easier to audit.

## Submit a record

Use the `/submit` page or the GitHub issue template. Include:

- A precise title, date or date range, location, and category.
- A factual summary of what happened.
- A separate outcome describing investigation, charges, judgment, reversal,
  or unresolved status.
- Direct URLs to the strongest available sources.
- The relevant actors, including a government, party, organisation, or
  institution only when the source supports that connection.

## Evidence rules

- **Official:** government, court, regulator, or statutory record.
- **Court-tested:** a case was litigated, charged, convicted, acquitted, or
  dismissed. State the exact outcome; do not turn an allegation into a verdict.
- **Reported:** credible newswire, rights group, or established publication.
- **Alleged:** a serious public allegation without a settled legal finding.
- **Opposition claim:** a political claim kept with clear attribution, not
  presented as established fact.

Wikipedia and social media are leads unless a separate source verifies the
claim. A pending citation may remain in the research queue, but it must be
labelled pending and must not be presented as a live source link.

## Corrections

Open an issue when a date, number, name, status, source, or wording is wrong.
Explain what should change and provide the replacement source. Court outcomes
and official records take priority over commentary and campaign material.

## Style

Use plain language. Avoid loaded labels, sweeping causal claims, and claims
about motive unless a source directly supports them. Keep criticism and praise
attributed. Record improvements and adverse outcomes with the same standard.

Run the validator before submitting:

```powershell
npm run validate
```
